How to Play

OBJECTIVE

Chess on a 9×8 board. You play as White against an AI opponent. Put the king in checkmate to win. The AI is rated at a fixed 600 ELO.

POLY-CHESS RULES
• Each side starts with 2 queens — one on each side of the king.
• The board is 9 columns wide, so each side has 9 pawns in the front row.
• All other standard chess rules apply — see below.
PLAYING
• Tap a piece to select it — legal moves are highlighted on the board.
• Tap a highlighted square to move. Tap the selected piece again to deselect.
SPECIAL MOVES
• Castling — King moves two squares toward a rook (neither can have moved; king cannot be in check, pass through check, or land in check).
• En passant — Capture a pawn that just moved two squares past yours, as if it had only moved one.
PROMOTION

When a pawn reaches the back rank, a promotion menu appears. Choose Queen, Rook, Bishop, or Knight.

DRAWS & ELO
• The game is drawn by stalemate, 50-move rule, threefold repetition, or insufficient material.
• Stalemate caused by the AI counts as a win for you, not a draw.
• Your ELO updates after every game (K-factor 32). The AI is fixed at 600.
